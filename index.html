Momentum

Momentum Gaps

Trade opening gaps that break structure; lean into early momentum with clear stops, targets, and gap logic.

The Edge

  • Stock has Pro/Novice Gap graded B or better
  • Void between congestion zones
  • Valid entry pattern
  • LOD/HOD initial stop
  • Target within ATR, sized appropriately

Pro Gap: Flash ORB

Set stop at LOD/HOD. Move as pattern confirms and pivots form.

Novice Gap: Curl

Reversal wedge pattern. Buy/Sell setup.

Criteria

  • Stock trending in direction of trade
  • Relative Strength/Weakness to market
  • Above/Below VWAP for longs/shorts

Entry

  • First candle to close on a new high
  • ORB: opening candle sets range, trade triggers on break
  • Stop-Out Reentry when stock full-stops initial entry
  • Above VWAP & 21EMA on 5-min
  • Low volume pullback / high volume breakout
  • Volume > 1M average daily

Exit

  • Low of previous candle after breakout
  • Critical structural levels (resistance or HOD)
  • Huge sellers on Level 2
  • Breakout or Bailout!

Risk Management

Risk per trade$150
Risk per day$450 or 3R
Targets1.5R with pivot SL management

Abort If

  • Heading into news event
  • Sudden volume dropoff
  • Abnormal market volatility against trade

Scalps

Extension Scalps

Quick mean-reversion scalps when price is stretched into structure; tiny targets, hard stops, no overnight.

Range Scalp

  • Stocks moving into trendlines of S/R in a channel, wedge, or triangle
  • Entry at touch of trendline for reversal
  • Targets: 2m 9EMA or 2m 21EMA / 5m 9EMA
  • Stop: prior pivot confirming breakout

Price-to-EMA gap MUST exceed trendline-to-pivot gap.

Extension Scalp

Needs to be extended. See criteria checklist below.

Rules

  • $1.50 HARD STOP on options scalps
  • If trade moves against you and comes back to BE, take BE
  • DO NOT hold scalps for a recovery
  • $3.00 down in one day = lockout
  • NO overnight holding
  • Do not scalp on earnings day
  • If 5m 9EMA catches up to your trade, exit — no longer extended
  • DO NOT scalp power trends (price hugging 9EMA on 1m)
  • Do not trade the first 5 minutes of price action

Scalp Criteria Checklist

Main Requirements
1Going into resistance/support on DAILY, 65 or 15 min
2Extended ≥5 green/red bars on 5min, >2-3 bars on 15min
3ATR is above 75%
Secondary Requirements
1Extended away from 9EMA on 5min & 15min (9EMA <50°)
2Higher timeframe in alignment + extended into S/R area
3Market bias in alignment
4Technical pattern present

Take Profit

  • VWAP (if nearest)
  • 9EMA on the 5-min
  • Whichever first hits $0.30/contract

Avoid

  • Do not take after 12:00 PM on any day
  • Avoid on Fridays
  • Do not take on power trend moves
  • Do not hold to end of day

Options

Wheel Strategy

Premium income with cash-secured puts and covered calls on solid names in sideways-to-mildly-bullish conditions.

Technical Analysis

  • Support/Resistance analysis
  • Ensure uptrending 50EMA — price pops above flat 50EMA at high volume
  • Delta close to 0.3-0.4 (lower premiums, less likely assignment)
  • Implied Volatility between 30-70%

Rules

  • Look for stocks moving sideways or slightly upward
  • If stock declining but good fundamentals — target strong support
  • VIX < 40%
  • High volume trading (>200K/day) for narrow premium spreads
  • Stock price > 50 EMA (bullish trend)
  • No upcoming earnings announcements
  • Avoid media-hyped tickers
  • No penny stocks <$5 (above $10 ideal), no low volume under 1M/day
  • ETFs fine but no leveraged or inverse ETFs

Post-Market Tracking

By 11:00 AM the trading is done. Data work begins.

  • Entry, stop, and exit
  • Setup and pattern type
  • Management decisions
  • Context and reasoning
  • Mistakes and missed opportunities
  • Execution quality
  • Emotional state, hesitation, or forcing
  • Free-form emotional journal after data logging

Weekly Review

Review all trades — winning and losing. Identify repeating strengths, mistakes, and patterns. Ask what was clean, what was forced, what kept repeating.

Monthly Review

Pull up winning and losing trades in chart format. Redraw lines by hand. Study structure in detail. Mark entries, exits, lines, targets, and problem areas.

Psychology

The Mental Game

The market does not care about you. Traders need only two things: calm and confidence.

Core Beliefs

  • Embrace probabilistic outcomes — no single trade is crucial
  • Maintain emotional equilibrium — let the plan dictate, not emotions
  • Accept risk and release control — focus on strategy, not the market
  • Detach from outcomes — focus on process and execution quality

Be the Casino, Not the Gambler

  • Be calm in a winning streak — wins are just as random as losses
  • Be confident during a losing streak — it doesn't destroy your edge
  • Every spin, roll, hand, or dice is a unique outcome

Self-Therapy
